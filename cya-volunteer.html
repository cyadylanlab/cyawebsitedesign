Coach & Volunteer

CYA runs on parents and neighbors who show up. Here's how to join them.

CYA runs on volunteers. Every coach on the field is a parent or neighbor who showed up because they wanted to, not because it's their job, and that's exactly what makes this program what it is. If you've ever thought about coaching, this is your sign.

You Don’t Need Soccer Experience

Most CYA coaches are parent volunteers, not former players. We back every coach with a pre- and mid-season coaching seminar run by our professional coaching staff, and we cover the cost if you want to pursue an official coaching license.

What Coaching Looks Like

Time commitment depends on the age group, younger divisions practice and play only on Saturdays, older divisions add one or two weeknight practices. You’ll get support from your division coordinator and CYA staff coaches throughout the season, you’re never figuring it out alone.

Other Ways To Help

Not ready to coach? CYA also needs help with team parent duties, field setup, referee assignments, and event support. There’s a role that fits whatever time you have.
